E X O

EXO started as a twelve member boy group formed by SM Entertainment in Seoul, South Korea. The South Korean-Chinese group debuted in 2012 with members separated into two subgroups, EXO-K and EXO-M, performing music in Korean and Mandarin, respectively. They debuted their first televised performance at SBS's annual Gayo Daejun event on December 29, 2011. Since debut, three members (Kris, Luhan, and Tao) left the group. Following their departure, EXO carried on with nine members.

EXO rose to fame with the release of their best-selling first album XOXO (2013), which contained the breakthrough hit "Growl". XOXO was a critical and commercial success, winning Disk Daesang at the 28th Global Disk Awards and Album of the Year at the 15th Mnet Asian Music Awards. It eventually went on to sell over one million copies, putting EXO as the best-selling Korean artist in twelve years and earning them the title of "King of Male Idol Groups" for achieving such an award as rookies. Overdose (2014) made EXO the highest-charting Korean male act on Billboard 200. EXO were later ranked the most influencial celebrity by Forbes Korea for the year 2014 and ranked as the most powerful celebrity for the year 2015.

EXO have been recipients of numerous awards from South Korea and mainland China. EXO has won over 60 music program awards and continue their success. Last year, Exodus album earned the largest first-week sales ever for a K-pop act on the Billboard 200. Their newly released EX'ACT album earns the group their first No. 1 on Billboard's World Digital Songs chart with "Monster" and "Lucky One."
